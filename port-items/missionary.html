Church Missionary Apps
Web & iOS Dev. 2013-2016.

From 2013 to 2016, I worked for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints developing applications for the missionary department. While I won't discuss or show specifics of the apps here (were only for private/internal use), I do want to make mention of some of the technologies I worked with.




Cordova Mobile Development

The first year or so I spent working on a hybrid HTML 5 Cordova app, targeting the Android and iOS platforms. We worked with a framework called Sencha Touch, used SASS precompiled CSS, and a WebSQL database. This app was primarily for offline use, and user could periodically sync to share data with our central servers. I spent a bit of time on our Java backend server, but primarily focused on front end feature development.

Web development

Following that, I developed a few Angular based web apps. During this time I had the opportunity to work more extensively with package managers like npm and Bower, which I came to love. We also used the super useful task runners Grunt and Gulp for various projects. My javascript unit testing framework of choice was Jasmine, but we also tinkered with others. Again we worked with mostly Java backend services, but I also had some exposure to Node.js backends.

Native iOS Development

Starting in fall of 2015, I moved into native iOS development with Swift. We're creating an offline mapping app that I'm extremely proud of. Working in the iOS environment has been a blast. While I appreciate the great things you can do with a Cordova/Phonegap app, there's a lot of power in working with the native SDK.
